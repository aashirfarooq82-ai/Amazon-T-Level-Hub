The Qualification

What Are T Levels?

The essentials — what you're signing up for and why it matters

Overview

The Short Version

T Levels are two-year technical qualifications for students aged 16–19. Designed with employers, they give you real, relevant skills — not just theory.

They carry up to 168 UCAS points, making them equivalent to 3 A Levels for university entry.

Structure

How Time Is Split

  • 80% classroom — technical knowledge and core skills
  • 20% industry — 315+ hours with a real employer
  • Exams + projects — assessed on knowledge and skills
Subjects

What Can You Study?

  • Digital & IT
  • Construction & Built Environment
  • Health & Science
  • Business & Finance
  • Creative & Design
  • Engineering & Manufacturing
  • Education & Early Years
  • Agriculture & Environment

Assessment

How Do You Pass?

What's actually assessed and what the grading looks like

Core Exams

Technical Knowledge

Written exams set by your awarding body (NCFE, BTEC, City & Guilds). You must pass these to achieve the overall T Level — they can't be skipped or substituted.

Practical Work

Occupational Specialism

Real-world tasks and projects that prove your skills in action. Graded at Pass, Merit, or Distinction — doing well here lifts your overall grade significantly.

Placement

Industry Hours

A minimum of 315 hours with a real employer. Attendance is tracked and non-negotiable — missing placement days puts your certificate at risk.

English & Maths

GCSE Requirements

If you don't already hold grade 4+ in GCSE English and Maths, you must continue working towards them alongside your T Level. Both are needed for the full certificate.

Grades

How You're Graded

  • Distinction* — highest grade
  • Distinction
  • Merit
  • Pass
Tips

How to Do Well

  • Never miss a placement day — absences are recorded
  • Keep a reflective journal throughout the placement
  • Be proactive — ask questions, take initiative
  • Start exam revision early — these are real exams
  • Talk to your tutor early if you're struggling

The Industry Placement

The 315-hour placement is what separates T Levels from every other post-16 qualification. Here's how it fits into two years.

Year 1

Mostly classroom-based. Build the knowledge foundation before entering the workplace.

315+

Minimum placement hours — roughly 45 full working days across the programme.

Year 2

More placement time. Apply what you've learned in a live professional environment.

Paid?

Some employers pay — not guaranteed, but worth asking your provider before you start.

FAQs

Common Questions

Things students ask us most

Can I go to university after a T Level?
Yes. T Levels carry up to 168 UCAS points — equivalent to 3 A Levels — and are accepted by most UK universities. Some courses have specific requirements, so always check directly. Many universities now actively seek T Level applicants because of the work experience they bring.
What if I don't enjoy my placement?
Speak to your tutor or provider as early as possible. Placements can sometimes be adjusted if there's a genuine issue. Raising concerns early is always better than struggling through — your provider wants the placement to work for you.
Do I get paid for the placement?
It depends on the employer. Some Amazon programmes include expenses or payment, but it's not a legal requirement. Ask your school or provider about the specific arrangement before you start.
Is a T Level harder than a BTEC?
Different rather than harder. T Levels have compulsory exams and a mandatory placement — BTECs don't always include either. Many students find the real-world, applied nature of T Levels more motivating, even if the exam component requires genuine preparation.
What happens if I fail part of the T Level?
You can resit exams — check with your awarding body for dates and rules. Not completing the 315 placement hours means no full T Level certificate, so attendance matters throughout.
Can I do an apprenticeship after a T Level?
Absolutely — many students do. Your T Level and Amazon placement put you in a strong position for higher and degree apprenticeships. Some Amazon placement students are offered apprenticeships directly with Amazon on completion.
