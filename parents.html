The Qualification

What Are T Levels?

The essentials every parent should know

Overview

A New Kind of Qualification

T Levels are two-year technical qualifications for 16–19 year olds. Co-designed with major employers, they combine rigorous classroom learning with a real industry placement.

They carry up to 168 UCAS points — equivalent to 3 A Levels — and are accepted by universities and employers across the UK.

Structure

How Time Is Split

  • 80% in the classroom — knowledge and technical skills
  • 20% industry placement — 315+ hours with a real employer
  • Assessed by exams and practical assignments
Outcomes

What Doors It Opens

  • University — up to 168 UCAS points
  • Higher or degree apprenticeship
  • Skilled employment directly
  • Progression within Amazon itself

The Amazon Placement

The industry placement is what makes T Levels different from any other post-16 qualification. Here is what your child will experience.

Year 1

Mostly classroom-based. Your child builds knowledge before entering the workplace.

315+

Minimum hours spent with Amazon — roughly 45 full working days across the programme.

Mentor

Amazon assigns each student a personal workplace mentor throughout the placement.

Year 2

More placement time, applying classroom learning in real Amazon projects and teams.

Your Role

How to Support Your Child

Practical ways to help throughout the T Level journey

Day to Day

Practical Support

  • Help plan travel to placement on time
  • Encourage them to write reflections after placement days
  • Ask about what they did — it builds confidence
  • Ensure appropriate attire for placement days
  • Support exam revision — these are real exams
Conversations

Talking About the Future

T Levels work best when students have a sense of direction. Open conversations about goals are one of the most valuable things you can do.

  • Ask what they enjoy most at Amazon
  • Discuss whether uni, apprenticeship, or work appeals
  • Celebrate workplace achievements — it is real work
Concerns

If Something Is Not Right

If your child is struggling — with the placement, the exams, or anything else — the first step is to contact their T Level tutor at school or college.

Amazon has a student wellbeing process and will work with the school to resolve issues early. No concern is too small to raise. The most important thing is that your child keeps attending and communicates openly.

FAQs

Parent Questions

What parents ask us most often

Is a T Level as good as A Levels?
T Levels and A Levels are equivalent in UCAS points (up to 168 each) and both accepted by the vast majority of UK universities. T Levels have the significant advantage of including real work experience. For students who know their industry and learn well through doing, a T Level can be a stronger choice than A Levels.
Will my child be safe and supported at Amazon?
Yes. Amazon has robust safeguarding and health & safety processes for all T Level students. Every student has a dedicated mentor, and the placement is coordinated with their school. Schools carry out health & safety checks before any student begins. If your child has any concerns, they can speak to their mentor, their tutor, or contact us directly.
Will my child be paid for the placement?
Payment is not a legal requirement for T Level placements and varies by programme. Some Amazon placements include expenses or payment — ask your child's school or provider about the specific arrangement before the placement begins.
What if my child does not enjoy their subject or placement?
Encourage your child to speak to their T Level tutor as early as possible. Placements can sometimes be adjusted if there is a genuine mismatch, and support can be put in place. The important thing is to raise concerns early rather than waiting.
Can my child still go to a top university after a T Level?
Most universities accept T Levels, including many Russell Group institutions. However, entry requirements vary by course and university, so it is important to check early. Some universities now actively seek T Level applicants because of the work experience they bring.
