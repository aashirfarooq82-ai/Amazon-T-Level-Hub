What to Look For

Not all T Level placements are the same. Here are the key factors that make a difference to student success.

Employer Partnerships

Look for colleges with established relationships with major employers like Amazon. These partnerships indicate quality placements and strong student support.

Student Outcomes

Check progression rates to university, apprenticeships, and employment. Colleges with strong Amazon partnerships often have higher placement completion rates.

FAQs

Common Questions

What students and parents ask about finding T Level providers

How do I know if a college offers Amazon placements?
Our search tool clearly marks colleges that offer Amazon placements. You can also look for the Amazon T Levels badge on college websites or contact them directly to ask about their industry placement partners.
Can I visit a college before applying?
Yes. Most colleges hold open days and taster sessions throughout the year. We recommend visiting at least two or three providers to compare facilities, speak to current students, and get a feel for the college environment.
What if there is no Amazon placement near me?
Amazon is expanding its placement network across the UK. If there is no local Amazon placement, speak to your school or target college about expressing interest. Amazon prioritises schools that actively engage with their T Level programme.
Are all T Level providers the same quality?
No. T Level quality varies by provider. Check Ofsted ratings, student satisfaction scores, and progression outcomes. Colleges with strong employer partnerships like Amazon typically have better resources and support for placements.
